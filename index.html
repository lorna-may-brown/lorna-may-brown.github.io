Lorna May Brown

MSc Researcher, Marine Biologist, Scuba Diving Instructor

First Impressions

A world traveller, with experience living in five different countries and visiting many more, Lorna is not only fluent in Spanish, but also highly multicultural and adaptable. Her passion for wildlife ecoloigy and conservation was nurtured from a young age, and then through seeking opportunities to interact with and conserve all manner of creatures, she soon found herself beginning her journey as a Marine Biologist.

Learn more

Education

Having completed secondary education in England, Lorna pursued her Bachelors of Science in Integrated Animal Biology at the University of South Florida after being granted an academic scholarship. Then, in 2020 she sought her Master's of Science in Marine Vertebrate Ecology and Conservation at the University of Exeter.

Learn more

SCUBA Diving

An avid SCUBA diver with over seven years experience diving in six countries, and a now certified PADI Master Scuba Diver Trainer. While diving has always been extremely enjoyable to Lorna, it has become more than a simple hobbie; its made her a more conscientious marine biologist and enabled underwater visual surveys for ecosystem monitoring. It has even become its own career adjacent through teaching.

Learn more

Key Projects and experience

Delve deeper into some of the projects and experiences that best illustrate Lorna's experience as a Marine Biologist. From her early years working in remote research hubs to her Master's dissertation: here is where you find it all.

Research Dissertation - University of Exeter

First nation-wide assessment of the abundance and distribution of three endangered sea turtle species in Belize, using a large dataset covering eight years of underwater visual censuses and baited remote underwater videos.

Learn more

Research Assistant - Ecology Project International

Lead research assistant helping efforts at Pacuare Reserve in Costa Rica to monitor nesting behaviours of three marine turtle species.

Learn more

Aquarist and Keeper - The Florida Aquarium

Assited the work of two different departments with a wide range of both terrestrial and aquatic species, developing skills in husbandry, operant conditioning and guest education.

Learn more

Small Cat Keeper - Big Cat Rescue

From the infamous 'Tiger King' series on Netflix, at Big Cat Rescue Lorna gained experience with husbandry and training with several species of sometimes aggresive wildcats in the heart of Florida.

Learn more

Research Assistant

Aided efforts to survey coral reef ecosystem around Cap Ternay, used in long-term monitoring and recovery research sponsored by the Seychellois government, through the use of underwater visual censuses.

Learn more

Animal Attendant

Supported the work of Ian Newby, founder of 'Dangerous Wild Animal Rescue Facility', one of the only UK facilities to help house many illegal, exotic and often dangerous animals, abandoned after failed domestication.
